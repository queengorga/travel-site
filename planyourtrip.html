Transportation

Metro

The Metro of Rome only has three lines and is currently the smallest metro system in Europe. Nevertheless, it reaches the city's most important landmarks. Opened in 1955, the metro (subway or underground) in Rome has grown very little by little and currently only operates 37,3 miles (60 km) made up of three lines. It is one of the smallest metros in Europe.

Bus

Rome has currently over 350 bus lines and it can sometimes be a bit confusing to find the right bus. Since the metro lines of Rome are rather limited, visitors will most likely need the bus public transport system to get to certain parts of the city.

Railway

The urban trains in Rome, called Ferrovie Urbane, complement the three metro lines and are run by the same company.Since the tickets are the same as other forms of public transportation in Rome, these can be bought in urban train stations, metro stations, news agents and convenience stores.
